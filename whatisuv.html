What are UV radiation and UV index?

Ultraviolet (UV) radiation is generated from the sun and enters our atmosphere. Overexposure to UV can cause damage to skin cells and lead to skin cancer.

The UV index is a measure of the level of UV radiation. It gives people an idea of how strong the UV radiation in the area. The scale is from 1 to 11.

Read more!

What is skin cancer?

Skin cancer can be caused by exposure to ultraviolet radiation, most commonly from the sun. Every time our skin is exposed to ultraviolet radiation, the structure of our skin cells changes.

There are three types of skin cancer, but all are caused when skin cells are damaged by ultraviolet radiation: Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC), Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC), Melanoma.
