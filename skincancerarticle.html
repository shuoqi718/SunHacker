Understanding skin cancer!

Overexposure to UV can cause damage to skin cells and lead to skin cancer.

How does skin work?

Your skin is your body's largest organ. It protects your body from the outside world, including sunlight, heat, injury and infection.

It also helps regulate your body's temperature, stores fat and water, and produces Vitamin D.

What is skin cancer?

Skin cancer can be caused by exposure to ultraviolet radiation, most commonly from the sun. Every time our skin is exposed to ultraviolet radiation, the structure of our skin cells changes.

Too much ultraviolet radiation exposure can cause irreversible damage to the DNA of skin cells, and one damaged cell can start a skin cancer growing

What are the types of skin cancer?

There are three types of skin cancer, but all are caused when skin cells are damaged by ultraviolet radiation: Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC), Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC), Melanoma

BCCs and SCCs are the most common skin cancers and are often grouped together as non-melanoma skin cancers. These cancers do not usually spread to other parts of the body, although they do require prompt treatment to prevent this from happening. They also tend to be less dangerous than melanoma.
