What is ultraviolet radiation?

Overexposure to UV can cause damage to skin cells and lead to skin cancer.

Ultraviolet (UV) radiation is generated from the sun and enters our atmosphere.

Even though the earth’s ozone layer reflects most of it, and cloud cover can stop it getting to the earth’s surface, some of it still reaches us.

How can we avoid UV overexposure?

The amount of ultraviolet radiation our skin receives is also influenced by the sun's position in the sky. The higher the sun, the higher the levels of ultraviolet radiation.

Most Australians' lifetime exposure to ultraviolet radiation happens during childhood to young adulthood. It’s important to protect your skin throughout your life but particularly during these life stages.

The effects of tanning build up over your lifetime, even if you haven’t been burnt often.

Remember, while you can be outside safely in the sun, overexposure without the necessary protection can cause serious damage to your skin and eyes.

Why is sun exposure a problem in Australia?

Because Australia is relatively close to the equator, we receive higher overall levels of ultraviolet radiation than countries such as Canada, Germany or the United Kingdom.
